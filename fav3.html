An Empathy-Driven, Conversational Artificial Intelligence Agent (Wysa) for Digital Mental Well-Being: Real-World Data Evaluation Mixed-Methods Study

Summary
Depression affects a significant portion of the global population, yet barriers like limited resources and stigma impede effective treatment. AI-enabled mobile apps offer a promising solution by providing accessible, empathetic support.

The study by Inkster et al. (2018) evaluates the effectiveness of one such app, Wysa, in alleviating symptoms of depression through real-world data analysis.
The study involved anonymous global users who voluntarily installed the Wysa app and engaged in text-based messaging, while self-reporting symptoms of depression using the Patient Health Questionnaire-9.

Based on their app usage patterns across two consecutive screening time points, two distinct user groups emerged: high users and low users. Employing a mixed-methods approach, the study evaluated the impact and engagement levels among these users. Quantitative analysis assessed the app's impact by comparing the average improvement in depression symptoms between high and low users, while qualitative analysis examined user engagement and experience through in-app feedback analysis. Additionally, the study evaluated the performance of a machine learning classifier to identify user objections during conversations.
The analysis of mood improvement between high (n=108) and low (n=21) users showed a significantly greater average improvement in the high users group compared to the low users group. This difference was statistically significant with a Mann-Whitney P-value of .03 and a moderate effect size of 0.63. Additionally, 67.7% of user-provided feedback responses indicated that the app experience was helpful and encouraging.
