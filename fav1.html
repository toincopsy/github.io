Summary
The study by Ly et al. (2014) compared the efficacy of two interventions (Behavioral Activation (BA) and Mindfulness-Based Treatment) for major depressive disorder (MDD), administered via smartphone. Given the prevalence of MDD and the accessibility of mobile technology, this research investigates whether these treatments can be effectively delivered via smartphones for people with MDD.
The study was a randomized controlled trial involving 81 participants (72 at post treatment analysis) diagnosed with MDD, comparing the effects of an 8-week BA program and a mindfulness program, both administered via smartphone applications. The effectiveness of these interventions was analyzed using the Beck Depression Inventory-II (BDI-II) and the Patient Health Questionnaire Depression Scale (PHQ-9) pre-treatment, post-treatment and 6 months after the treatment.
The findings revealed no significant differences between the two interventions in reducing depressive symptoms overall. There was no significant interaction effect of group and time on the PHQ-9 and the BDI-II nether for comparing pre- and post-treatment nor for comparing pre-treatment and the follow up after 6 month.

However, subgroup analyses indicated that from pre-treatment to the follow up after 6 month from BA was more effective for participants with higher initial severity of depression, whereas the mindfulness treatment showed better outcomes for those with lower initial severity. This suggests that treatment effectiveness may vary based on the baseline severity of depression, highlighting the importance of personalized treatment approaches in mental health interventions.
